We will learn grammar in a very interesting way. Let us have a look to our grammar family.

The Grammar Family

There is a family in London whose surname is Grammar. There is a couple, Mr. Noun and Mrs. Verb. The couple has three children: a son, Pronoun and two daughters, Adverb and Adjective. The son (pronoun) has to do all the work of his father in his absence. The two daughters love each other but there is a difference in them Adjective loves her mother more and she always modifies her when there is a need. There are two servants in the family. Preposition and Conjunction. The preposition is the chief servant. He is the official servant of his master. Conjunction is the family servant and lookd after every member of the family. The Interjection joins the family in times of joy and sorrow.

There are total eight parts of speech which are as follows :

  1. Noun
  2. Pronoun
  3. Adjective
  4. Verb
  5. Adverb
  6. Preposition
  7. Conjuction
  8. Interjection


Noun:

Noun is the name of the person, animal, place, or things.
Ex: Ram, Shyam, Dog, Cat, Mumbai, Delhi, Knife, Table, etc.

  • Ex: Ram is a good boy.
  • I have a Dog.
  • I live in Mumbai.
  • I like to read books.


Pronoun:

It is a word which is used in a place of a noun.
Ex: I, We, He, She, It They, Me, My, You,Them, etc.

  • Iam a good boy.
  • He like to swim.
  • You are good boy.
  • They are good players.


Adjective:

An Adjective is a word which qualifies a noun and pronoun.
Ex: Good, Bad, Red, White, Tall, Short, Black, Thin, Thick, Beautiful, Ugly, etc.

  • Ram has a beautiful Car.
  • They are bad players.
  • The Cricket Stadium is very big.
  • Coal is black in colour.


Verb:

A verb is a word which denotes an action or state of being.
Ex: Read, Write, Throw, Catch, Jump, Sing, Play, Laugh, etc.

  • Harry is reading a book.
  • Bird is flying in the sky.
  • Johnson is throwing a ball.
  • He is writing a letter.


Adverb:

An Adverb is a word which qualifies a verb and adjective.
Ex: Slowly, Carefully, Neatly, Confidently, etc.

  • He is writing a letter slowly.
  • Watch this show carefully.
  • Make this painting neatly.
  • An Athelete is running fastly.


Preposition:

A preposition is a word which tells us about the direction of a noun, pronoun.
Ex: Up, Down, On, Before, After, Inside, Outside, etc.

  • He is swimming inside the water.
  • The book is on the table.
  • He comes before me.
  • It is raining outside.


Conjuction:

A conjunction is a word which joins the two sentence together.
Ex: And, But, Or, etc.
  • I want a car but I don't have money.
  • He is my best friend therefore I supported him.
  • He is working hard hence he got success.
  • He finished the match and I left the match.


Interjection:

An Interjection is a word which express the feelings and emotion.
Ex: Wow!, Ahh!, Ouchh!, Hurrey!, etc.

  • Wow! what a wonderful flower it is.
  • Hurrey! we won the match.
  • Ouchh! it is so hard.
  • Ahh! It is so cool today.
